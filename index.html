Academic Research · 2024

Brand Identity and Its Impact on Customer Loyalty & Repurchase Intention: A Study of Georgian Supermarket Chains

In this research, I explored how brand identity influences customer perception, loyalty, and repurchase decisions in Georgia's competitive FMCG retail sector. The study compares two market segments: mass-market chains ("Ori Nabiji" and "Magnet") and premium chains ("Gastronomi" and "Europrodukti"), analyzing visual communication, tone of voice, pricing strategy, and service standards.

Using a mixed-methods approach, I combined a quantitative survey (100 respondents), expert interviews with marketing managers, and in-depth social media content analysis. The findings highlight significant differences in how loyalty is built across segments: price and habit dominate in mass-market, while emotional connection, quality perception, and lifestyle alignment drive loyalty in the premium segment.

This thesis demonstrates my ability to conduct structured academic research, analyze real market dynamics, and translate insights into practical branding and marketing recommendations.
